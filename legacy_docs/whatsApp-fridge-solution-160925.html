1. PURPOSE OF THIS AGREEMENT

1.1 This Heads of Agreement (“HoA”) sets out the understanding between the Provider and the Client regarding the design, build, and pilot of a WhatsApp workflow solution (the “Project”).
1.2 Certain terms are intended to be binding (see clause 2.1) and others non-binding (see clause 2.2).

2. BINDING VS NON-BINDING SECTIONS

2.1 Binding Clauses: Confidentiality, Intellectual Property, Data Protection, Compliance with Meta/WhatsApp policies, Limitation of Liability, Governing Law & Dispute Resolution, Costs, Notices, Term & Termination.
2.2 Non-Binding Clauses: Scope, Timelines, Commercials, Acceptance, Change Control, Service Levels (except as expressly agreed in Schedule 5). These reflect current intentions but will be confirmed in a definitive agreement.

3. SCOPE AND DELIVERABLES

3.1 The Provider will deliver the scope described in Schedule 1.
3.2 Client responsibilities are set out in Schedule 2.

4. COMMERCIALS

4.1 The Provider will fund and carry all costs of developing and delivering the Project during the build and pilot phases.
4.2 The Client will not be charged any Build Fee.
4.3 Subscription fees for ongoing use of the solution will be agreed before the end of the Trial Period, based on observed usage, team size, broadcast volume, and scaling prospects.
4.4 Pass-through costs (including Meta/WhatsApp messaging fees, QR code printing/deployment, and third-party translation vendor costs if required) are for the Client’s account.
4.5 All prices exclude VAT, which will apply where relevant.

5. TIMELINES & MILESTONES

5.1 Target dates are set out in the Key Terms Table.
5.2 Dates are dependent on Client approvals and Meta template approvals.

6. ACCEPTANCE

6.1 Acceptance criteria per deliverable will be confirmed in checklists.
6.2 A deliverable will be deemed accepted if not rejected (with reasons) in writing within 5 business days.

7. CHANGE CONTROL

7.1 Any change to scope, cost, or time must be recorded in a signed Change Note.

8. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

8.1 Each party retains ownership of pre-existing IP.
8.2 Upon full payment of any subscription fees due, the Provider grants the Client a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive licence to use the Deliverables for its internal business purposes.
8.3 The Provider retains ownership of its platform components, frameworks, and reusable libraries.

9. DATA PROTECTION (POPIA) & SECURITY

9.1 Client is the Responsible Party; Provider is the Operator.
9.2 The parties will comply with the Data Processing Addendum (Schedule 4).
9.3 Breach notification: within 72 hours.
9.4 Regional hosting preference: South Africa (or nearest reputable region if unavailable).

10. COMPLIANCE WITH META/WHATSAPP POLICIES

10.1 Client content and templates must comply with Meta’s policies.
10.2 Client approves all outbound templates.
10.3 The Provider is not responsible for Meta approval timelines or per-message charges.

11. SERVICE LEVELS (TRIAL)

11.1 Support during trial: reasonable endeavours within business hours (SAST).
11.2 Incident response targets are in Schedule 5.
11.3 Post-trial SLAs to be agreed before subscription begins.

12. WARRANTIES & DISCLAIMERS

12.1 Each party warrants it has authority to sign.
12.2 The Provider warrants delivery with reasonable skill and care.
12.3 No warranty is given for third-party platforms (Meta/WhatsApp).

13. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

13.1 No party is liable for indirect or consequential losses.
13.2 Liability is capped at the greater of:

  • (a) fees paid in the prior 12 months, or
  • (b) the subscription fees payable in the first 12 months following the Trial Period.
    13.3 Exclusions: confidentiality breaches, IP infringement, data protection breaches.

14. CONFIDENTIALITY

14.1 Each party will keep information confidential.
14.2 Obligations survive for 5 years after termination.

15. TERM & TERMINATION

15.1 This HoA is effective from signature until replaced by a definitive agreement or terminated.
15.2 Either party may terminate for material breach, with 10 business days’ cure period.
15.3 On termination:

  • Outstanding fees must be settled
  • Provider will hand over Client data
  • Confidentiality and Data Protection obligations survive

16. GOVERNING LAW & DISPUTE RESOLUTION

16.1 This HoA is governed by South African law.
16.2 Disputes will be escalated: negotiation → mediation → [litigation/arbitration].

17. COSTS & EXCLUSIVITY

17.1 Each party bears its own costs in negotiating this HoA.
17.2 No exclusivity applies unless otherwise agreed in writing.

18. NOTICES

18.1 Formal notices must be sent to the addresses in the Key Terms Table, with email copies.
